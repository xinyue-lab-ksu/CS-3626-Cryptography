# CS-3626-Cryptography

This repository contains open-access undergraduate course materials for **CS 3626: Cryptography**. The course introduces the mathematical and practical foundations of cryptography, including historical ciphers, symmetric encryption, public-key cryptography, hash functions, message authentication, digital signatures, and common attack strategies with countermeasures.

The materials are designed for undergraduate students in computer science, cybersecurity, software engineering, and related fields.

---

## Course Overview

Cryptography is the study of methods for protecting information against unauthorized access, modification, and misuse. This course covers both the theory and practice of cryptographic systems. Students will learn the mathematical foundations needed to understand cryptographic algorithms and will also gain experience with basic implementation concepts.

Major topics include:

* Introduction to cryptographic concepts
* Modular arithmetic and number theory
* Historical cryptography
* Symmetric encryption and block ciphers
* DES and AES
* Finite fields
* Modes of operation
* Stream ciphers
* Public-key cryptography
* RSA, Diffie-Hellman, ElGamal, and elliptic curve cryptography
* Cryptographic hash functions
* Message authentication codes
* Digital signatures
* Common attacks and countermeasures

---

## Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

1. Explain the concepts, components, methodology, and architecture of cryptography.
2. Discuss different foundations of cryptosystems.
3. Compare and contrast symmetric and asymmetric cryptography, including their advantages and limitations.
4. Explain vulnerability analysis and different layers of attacks.
5. Implement text conversion for cryptographic foundations.
6. Implement basic cryptographic algorithms.
